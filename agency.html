// Field Actions: San Francisco / Bay Area

Ten ways to put a value into practice this week, each tied to a real group already operating in the Bay Area.

Action
Pick one regular purchase and move it to a local or worker-owned business.
Network of Bay Area Worker Cooperatives: nobawc.org
A network of worker-owned businesses across the Bay, from Mandela Grocery to Rich City Rides.
Action
Sit in on a session that decides how city money gets spent.
Participatory Budgeting Oakland: pboakland.org
Residents vote directly on how a slice of city money is spent.
Action
See how community-owned land and housing works.
Bay Area Community Land Trust: bayareaclt.org · East Bay Permanent Real Estate Cooperative: ebprec.org
Models that keep housing affordable by holding land off the speculative market.
Action
Borrow or build something instead of buying it.
Oakland Tool Lending Library: oaklandlibrary.org/otll · Berkeley Tool Lending Library: berkeleypubliclibrary.org
Thousands of tools, free with a library card; access without ownership (Berkeley's has run since 1979).
Action
Sign up and offer one thing you could teach or do.
Bay Area Community Exchange (BACE) Timebank: sfbace.org
A free network trading skills using time as currency: an hour you give earns an hour you can spend, everyone's hour equal.
Action
Volunteer a shift or donate food and supplies.
GLIDE: glide.org
SF institution since 1963; its Daily Free Meals program serves ~1,700 meals a day, plus crisis, women's, and youth services.
Action
Join a mutual-aid pod, or start a borrow / gift network on your block.
SF Bay Mutual Aid: sfmutualaid.com · Buy Nothing Project: buynothingproject.org
Neighbors meeting each other's needs directly, outside the market.
Action
Repair something broken instead of replacing it.
Fixit Clinic: fixitclinic.org · StopWaste RE:Source: resource.stopwaste.org
Guided community repair, plus an East Bay directory for reuse and repair.
Action
Make something from salvaged materials instead of buying new.
SCRAP: scrap-sf.org
A 50-year creative reuse depot that diverts materials from landfill and sells them cheap (moving to Bayview, Aug 2026).
Action
Find your nearest food bank, fridge, or eviction-defense network, just so you know it's there.
findhelp.org / 211 Bay Area
A searchable directory of local food, housing, and health resources.
