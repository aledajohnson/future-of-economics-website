The Map

Three kinds of economic thinking

These three categories address different levels of the same conversation. Understanding how they differ helps clarify why people often talk past each other in debates about the economy.

Economic Systems ask who owns the means of production and who makes decisions. Policy Schools debate how to manage an economy within a given system, usually capitalism. Economic Frameworks step back to ask what the economy should be for in the first place, and how we should measure whether it is working. Most public debate happens in the middle layer while the other two go largely unexamined.
